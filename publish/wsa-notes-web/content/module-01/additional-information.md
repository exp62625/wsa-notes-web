# Additional Information (Phase One)

## Overview
- This lesson is the printable cheat sheet for Phase One: it tells you **when** to trade (session clocks), **what** to trade (pair short list), and **how** to account for costs before you click buy/sell.
- London (03:00 AM EST / 10:00 MT4) and New York (08:00 AM EST / 15:00 MT4) are the only sessions Cue wants you focused on because they deliver the cleanest EUR/GBP and USD volatility.
- The supporting notes remind you how spread, commission, and position size interact so you do not underestimate risk when scaling volume.

## Setup
1. **Session clocks**
   - Post the session printable (`content/module-01/assets/phase-one-additional-information-session-wheel.jpeg`) next to your desk.
   - Highlight London 03:00 AM EST (10:00 MT4) and New York 08:00 AM EST (15:00 MT4); treat them as mandatory trading windows.
2. **Volatility map**
   - London session = EUR & GBP movers.
   - New York session = USD movers.
   - Asian session = JPY focus; Sydney = AUD focus (useful for context but not the core focus hours).
3. **Pair short list**
   - Majors: EURUSD (extra volatile), GBPUSD, USDJPY (Cue's primary), AUDUSD, USDCAD.
   - Crosses/commodities: EURCAD, EURGBP, EURAUD, XAUUSD.
4. **Risk template**
   - Cue's baseline: 0.50 lot per $500 balance, 1.00 lot per $1,000 (adjust but know this benchmark).
   - Remember: Spread × lot size = immediate negative P/L; add commissions for true starting drawdown.

## Execution Rules
1. **Trade when your pair is awake**
   - Only take EUR/GBP setups during London hours and USD setups during New York to ride actual volume spikes.
2. **Do the cost math before entries**
   - Compute (spread × lot size) + commissions so you know the hole you must climb out of; size accordingly.
3. **One-pair mastery**
   - Pick the pair whose active session fits your lifestyle and stay with it until you can anticipate its rhythm daily.
4. **Volume vs. time in market**
   - Smaller volume = longer holds to see meaningful profits; larger volume = quicker paydays but bigger emotional swings—manage risk so you can exploit both when appropriate.
5. **Session-aligned journaling**
   - Tag every screenshot and note with the session so you can see if off-session trades are costing you.

## Common Mistakes
- Trading sleepy sessions (e.g., running GBP setups during Asia) and blaming strategy instead of timing.
- Pair hopping every day, which prevents you from reading the nuances of any single market.
- Ignoring spread/commission so the trade starts deeper in drawdown than expected.
- Oversizing lots relative to balance just because spreads look small.
- Forgetting that higher volume tightens the profit window—leading to premature exits when price only needs a few micro-pips.

## Drills / Reps
1. **Session audit** – Review the last two weeks of trades and color-code winners/losers by session to prove why London/NY should be mandatory.
2. **Spread math reps** – For each pair on the short list, record the live spread, multiply by 0.50 / 1.00 lots, and log the actual starting drawdown.
3. **Pair journal** – Pick your primary pair, screenshot the first hour of its active session for ten days straight, and annotate recurring behaviors (wick traps, volume surges, etc.).
4. **Risk template rehearsal** – Before live trading, simulate five entries using Cue's lot-per-balance rule and note how long (in pips/time) it takes to dig out of the initial spread.

## Questions to Revisit
- **Which session fuels USD volatility and what MT4 time matches it?** → New York session, 08:00 AM EST / 15:00 MT4.
- **If I'm focusing on JPY, when should I be awake?** → Asian session is the cleanest window; plan for the Tokyo open if you need that context, but still prioritize London/NY for core trades.
- **How do spread and commission change my break-even?** → Multiply spread by your lot size, add broker commission, and treat that as mandatory profit before moving your stop to break-even.
- **Why stay loyal to one pair?** → Mastery allows you to anticipate volatility cycles, news reactions, and session quirks, which is impossible when rotating through the entire watchlist daily.
