# Drawing Trendlines 2.0 (Phase One)

## Overview
- Trendlines are active tools, not decorations: point A + point B only establish the slope, point C is where the trade happens.
- Keep charts clean by color-coding TLs by timeframe (daily = red, H4 = green, H1 = orange, everything else = blue) so you always know the context of the line you are leaning on.
- Momentum analysis, moving averages, and Fibonacci behavior all work together with trendlines to define buy zones, sell zones, and confirmation candles.

## Setup
1. **Timeframe scan**
   - Start on the daily to mark any practical TLs (skip ones miles away from current price).
   - Drop to H4/H1 to capture the active slope and the most recent steep swings.
2. **Anchor selection**
   - Use the steepest meaningful wicks for point A and point B.
   - Align TLs with MA angles when possible; the MA slope often hints at which wicks share the same flow.
3. **Color + label**
   - Apply the color scheme above; label long-term TLs so you remember their origin when drilling down.
4. **Structure context**
   - Plot nearby horizontal levels (prior highs/lows, consolidation shelves) to frame buy/sell zones.
   - Overlay Fib swings (bullish-to-bullish, bearish-to-bearish) so you know whether price is in continuation (23.6%) or pullback territory (38.2%+).

## Execution Rules
1. **Maintain the live slope**
   - Keep modifying the TL as new wicks print; only delete when structure truly shifts.
   - A valid TL should keep price on one side and stay angled (avoid perfectly flat lines unless the market itself has stalled).
2. **Point C entries**
   - Wait for price to tap or closely respect the TL—server spreads mean “close enough” counts.
   - Combine the touch with Fib alignment (e.g., 23.6% continuation) and MA support/resistance.
3. **Buy vs. sell zones**
   - Above the main TL = buy zone; below = sell zone.
   - Use counter-trend TLs inside a zone to refine entries; the break + backside retest often trims drawdown.
4. **Counter-trend break confirmation**
   - Need a close beyond the counter-trend line, a sexy candle (long wick rejecting the prior direction) under/over the MAs, and preferably a retest that holds below/above the previous swing.
5. **Sexy candle criteria**
   - Long wick in the direction of rejection, body closing with momentum (bearish body for shorts, bullish for longs).
   - Ideally forms after multiple failed pushes at the same level, signaling exhaustion.
6. **Trend continuation management**
   - Trail the TL forward: each new higher low / lower high gives a new point B.
   - Stay in the trade as long as price respects the TL + MAs and Fib pullbacks do not violate the prior swing.

## Checklist
- [ ] Daily/H4/H1 TLs drawn and color-coded.
- [ ] Horizontal levels plotted around current price.
- [ ] Active Fib swing marked with 23.6 / 38.2 zones identified.
- [ ] Buy and sell zones labeled relative to the main TL.
- [ ] Counter-trend TL in place to guide entries.
- [ ] Confirmation line-up: TL touch, Fib context, MA alignment, sexy candle or comparable rejection.
- [ ] Risk defined (stop beyond prior wick / 100 pts on US30 example) and drawdown scenario understood.

## Common Mistakes
- Drawing TLs with the ray tool so they clutter future price action and hide new information.
- Anchoring on random bodies instead of the steepest relevant wicks, leading to false touches.
- Entering off point A or B instead of waiting for point C.
- Refusing to modify the TL as structure evolves, which turns a winning slope into noise.
- Ignoring MA slope/Fib context—trendlines alone do not tell you if the move is continuation or a fade.
- Taking counter-trend breaks without a sexy candle or without price clearing the previous micro-structure high/low.

## Drills
1. **Color-coded replay**: On replay mode, build daily → H4 → H1 TL stacks, label them, and pause before each would-be point C to journal the confluence.
2. **Counter-trend break log**: Record ten instances where a counter-trend TL broke—note whether a sexy candle + MA break + Fib position were present and how much drawdown followed.
3. **Fib alignment sprints**: For five trending legs, plot Fib after each new high/low and document how 23.6% vs. 38.2% behavior affected TL confidence.
4. **Buy/Sell zone tagging**: Screenshot recent sessions, shade buy vs. sell zones, and annotate which confirmations you waited for (or skipped) before taking entries.
