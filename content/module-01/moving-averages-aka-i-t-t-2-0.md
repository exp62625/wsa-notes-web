# Moving Averages (AKA ITT) 2.0 (Phase One)

## Overview
- Two simple moving averages (fast **SMA 6** in black, slow **SMA 85** in green) act as Cue's dynamic support/resistance rails and the quickest way to read trend strength.
- Where price sits relative to the stack tells you who has momentum: price **below** both lines = sustained selling pressure, **above** = bullish flow, **threading in-between** = consolidation you should ignore or zoom out from.
- The moving-average flow is timeframe-dependent. Each session you hunt for the chart (Daily, H4, H1, sometimes M30) where the lines arc cleanly instead of chopping the candles.
- MAs are never used alone—they reinforce fib retracements (23.6/38.2), structure, and trendlines so you only deploy entries when the entire story aligns.

## Setup
1. **Workspace / tools**
   - TradingView (or equivalent) with two simple moving averages: SMA 6 (black, medium thickness) and SMA 85 (green, medium thickness). Leave every other setting at default.
   - Candle chart as primary view; keep fib retracement tool, horizontal levels, and trendlines handy for confluence.
2. **Pre-trade context**
   - Start on the **Daily** to understand broad bias, then sweep H4 and H1 to see where the MAs give the cleanest read.
   - Note whether the slow (85) is acting as a drifting channel the market respects or whether price is whipping through both lines (a warning to stand down or step up a timeframe).
   - Mark recent swing points so you can sync fib anchors with MA touches for future pullback entries.
3. **Annotation standards**
   - Screenshot/save the timeframe where MAs are the smoothest and label it as the "reference MA chart" for the session.
   - Tag MA-based levels ("MA bounce", "MA break + retest") directly on the chart to review the behavior later.
   - Log whether price respected the 6 first or needed the deeper test into the 85 so you know which line has authority right now.

## Execution Rules
1. **Define directional bias**
   - Only look for sells when both SMAs sit **above** price and slope downward; only look for buys when they sit **below** price and slope upward.
   - If the fast SMA keeps piercing through the slow SMA and both weave through price, treat it as chop until a higher timeframe realigns them.
2. **Map structure with the MAs**
   - Combine MA location with classical structure: lower highs forming underneath the 6/85 stack confirm bearish sequences; higher lows built on top of the stack confirm bullish sequences.
   - Use trendlines to highlight when price is compressing into the MAs for a potential breakout.
3. **Plan entries off dynamic support/resistance**
   - During trends, wait for price to pull back into the fast SMA (6) first; require fib confluence (23.6% for momentum continuations, 38.2%+ for deeper corrections) and rejection wicks.
   - If the 6 breaks but the 85 still holds, treat the 85 as your last line—only engage if structure stays intact and momentum candles return in the trend direction.
4. **Spot transitions early**
   - Momentum shift = decisive candle close through the slow SMA **plus** a structural break (higher high after bearish swing or vice versa). Do not flip bias until both criteria print.
   - Failing to make new highs/lows while the opposite side of the MA stack keeps getting tagged is your cue to scale down risk or exit.

## Checklist
- [ ] SMA 6 & 85 plotted correctly on the session's cleanest timeframe (not just default H1).
- [ ] Bias aligns across higher timeframe (Daily/H4) and execution timeframe (H1/M30).
- [ ] Price is trending away from the MA stack, not chopping inside it.
- [ ] Entry plan ties together MA touch, structure level, and fib confluence.
- [ ] Exit/invalidations defined (e.g., candle close through slow SMA or failure to hold most recent swing).

## Common Mistakes
- Trading when the MAs are braided through price instead of waiting for a timeframe where they act as rails.
- Mixing indicator types (e.g., EMA vs SMA) or wrong lengths, which ruins the way Cue reads slope and spacing.
- Assuming one timeframe will always work; the market can force you to rotate between Daily, H4, H1, or M30 for clarity.
- Buying when MAs sit overhead or selling when they're under price just because you spotted a candle pattern.
- Ignoring structure/Fib context and treating MA touches as automatic entries.

## Drills / Reps
1. **Timeframe Scan Drill** – Every morning, cycle through Daily → M30 on one pair and document which timeframe shows the cleanest MA flow plus a short note why.
2. **MA Bias Journal** – Before each trade, log where price sits relative to both SMAs, what fib level aligns, and whether you're following or fading momentum. Review at week's end for rule violations.
3. **Transition Replay** – Use bar replay to mark the first candle that fully closes through the slow SMA and track what structure confirmed the reversal. Repeat until you can label transitions in real time.

## Questions to Revisit
- Which timeframe currently gives the cleanest read on the 6/85 flow, and does it agree with the higher-timeframe bias?
- Are the MAs acting as reliable dynamic support/resistance or are they chopping—meaning I should stand down?
- What structural or fib confluence do I have at the next MA touch, and what invalidates the idea if price knifes through the line?
