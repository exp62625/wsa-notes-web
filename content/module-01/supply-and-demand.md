# Supply and Demand (Phase One)

## Overview
- Treat supply/demand as "level two" structure: instead of a single price line you box the area where imbalance forced price to sprint away (rally-base-drop or drop-base-rally).
- Zones highlight where unfilled orders are likely waiting, so the first revisit of the zone is expected to stall or reverse price.
- Combining these blocks with the classic support/resistance map gives clearer confluence for Phase One continuation or reversal plays.

## Setup
1. **Workspace / tools** – Clean 4H or 1H charts, rectangle tool for zones, standard S/R template so you can see when a zone overlaps a prior line.
2. **Pre-trade context** – Run a top-down sweep (HTF → execution TF) to confirm the market just left an aggressive breakout or rejection, then log the direction you expect on the retest.
3. **Annotation standards** – Box zones from the *prior wick* to the *breakout candle body*, extend them forward, and label each as "fresh" (no touches), "2nd touch", etc. so you remember which ones are still high quality.

## Execution Rules
1. **Identify the breakout** – Spot the impulse candle that left a base (sideways candles) with speed; confirm it created new structure and not chop.
2. **Draw the zone correctly** – Anchor the rectangle from the previous candle's wick extreme to the body of the breakout candle (top-to-body for supply, wick-to-body upward for demand) and drag it into the future.
3. **Trade the first touch** – Plan to engage only on the first revisit of that zone; expect the initial touch to reject price and produce your entry signal.
4. **Combine with S/R** – If the zone overlaps a higher-timeframe line, weight it heavier and treat the confluence as the trigger area for continuation or reversal plays.
5. **Invalidate quickly** – If price slices straight through the full zone or if the zone has already produced multiple touches, stand down; the order imbalance is likely gone.

## Checklist
- [ ] Chart is on 4H or 1H with the breakout clearly visible.
- [ ] Zone is drawn from wick to breakout body and marked as "first touch".
- [ ] Directional bias matches the impulse that created the zone.
- [ ] Confluence with prior support/resistance or trend context is logged.
- [ ] Clear invalidation (clean break past the opposite edge of the zone) is defined before entry.

## Common Mistakes
- Treating zones like thin lines and ignoring the full range where orders sit.
- Trading second, third, or fourth touches even though the lesson stresses the first touch is the one with edge.
- Drawing zones on random timesteps (e.g., 5m) instead of the prescribed 4H/1H views, which makes them noisy and unreliable.
- Forgetting to incorporate broader support/resistance, leaving you with isolated zones that do not match the market's real battle lines.

## Drills / Reps
1. **Zone catalog** – Each day, mark five fresh rally-base-drop and drop-base-rally zones on 4H charts, label their status, and screenshot them for review.
2. **First-touch tracking** – Log the reaction of the first touch for every zone you marked (reject, chop, break) and tally the win rate after one week.
3. **Confluence overlay** – Once per trading session, overlay your supply/demand boxes on top of your S/R map and note how often they stack; review mismatches every Friday.

## Questions to Revisit
- What clues in the breakout candle tell me the imbalance is strong enough to expect a first-touch rejection?
- How many touches are too many before I retire a zone, and does the type of touch (wick vs. body close) matter?
- When a zone overlaps a classic support/resistance line, do I tighten my stop, widen it across the entire zone, or scale position size differently?
