# Support and Resistance (Phase One)

## Overview
- Quillen's original Support & Resistance HD lesson is about making the higher-timeframe structure painfully obvious before any entries.
- The workflow forces you to begin on the Daily line chart so every horizontal level is tied to multiple rejections ("struggle" zones) instead of isolated wicks.
- Color-coding each timeframe (Daily = red, H4 = green, H1 = orange, intraday random adjustments = blue) keeps the markup readable for a week+ while you layer later modules (supply/demand, trendlines, etc.).

## Setup
1. **Workspace / tools** – TradingView (or any platform with line + candle views), horizontal ray tool, Daily/H4/H1 charts, default EMA/SMA/ADX template loaded but temporarily hidden while marking.
2. **Pre-trade context** – Always start at the Daily line chart to lock directional bias, drop to H4 to stay within the current price range, then H1 for polishing. Ignore price action that sits far outside the active dealing range.
3. **Annotation standards** – Daily horizontals in red, H4 in green, H1 refinements in orange, ad-hoc levels (news spikes, session ranges) in blue. Label medians if you split a range; keep notes on how many touches validated each line.

## Execution Rules
1. **Daily line-chart sweep** – Stay on the line chart, drag red horizontals only where price repeatedly stalled. Prioritize clusters of closes rather than single wick stabs.
2. **Only relevant price action** – If the level lives 6–8 months back or beyond the current swing, skip it. Everything you draw must matter to the active trading range.
3. **H4 green refinement** – Still on the line chart, fill any "gaps" between daily lines by marking spike-heavy indecision zones. Too much empty space usually means you missed a level.
4. **Switch to candles on H1** – Flip back to candlesticks, paint orange medians that cut through the densest set of wicks. Adjust until each line touches multiple wick tips without slicing through bodies unnecessarily.
5. **Validate breadth** – For every new level ask: did price act as both support and resistance, or does it box the range you're trading? If not, delete it.
6. **Preserve and maintain** – A clean markup should last about a week and a half. Update only the zones that break or become irrelevant; don't nuke the entire chart each session.

## Checklist
- [ ] Daily line chart pass complete (red levels labeled by touches).
- [ ] H4 green levels fill every relevant gap near current price.
- [ ] H1 orange medians aligned with wick clusters, not random bodies.
- [ ] EMA/SMA/ADX template toggled back on after levels are locked.
- [ ] Markup saved for future modules (supply/demand, fibs, etc.).

## Common Mistakes
- Chasing historical levels far from current price and cluttering the chart.
- Drawing on candles first, which hides how often closes actually respected the zone.
- Leaving giant gaps between horizontals (usually signals a missed level).
- Redrawing the entire map every day and losing continuity in your analysis.

## Drills / Reps
1. **Daily-to-H4 sweep** – Pick a fresh FX pair, build Daily red levels, then immediately drop to H4 and fill any missing zones. Log how many green lines you added.
2. **Median hunt on H1** – Practice sliding an orange line until it grazes the highest number of wick tips inside a consolidation. Record before/after screenshots.
3. **One-week maintenance audit** – Keep the same markup for five sessions, only adjusting broken or irrelevant levels. Journal what held vs. what failed.

## Questions to Revisit
- Did each level act as both support and resistance at least once?
- Is this level still relevant to the current price or am I married to history?
- After toggling indicators back on, do the EMAs/SMA agree with the level's bias?
